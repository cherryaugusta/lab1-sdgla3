Engineering approach

Why workflow-heavy systems are at the centre of the portfolio

I build software with the strongest emphasis on systems that need to be understandable under review. Across the portfolio, that means backend services, typed frontend integration, explicit workflow state, audit trails, evaluation visibility, grounded retrieval, approval controls, and conservative handling of AI-assisted behaviour.

The most representative repositories are not generic CRUD demos. They are workflow-heavy systems where evidence, decisions, and operational state need to stay inspectable. That includes regulated-style review flows, retrieval-backed answer generation, model governance controls, and frontend control-plane design for constrained decision support.

A second recurring theme is maintainability. Even in smaller Python projects, I focus on service boundaries, validation, reproducibility, readable repository structure, and testable behaviour rather than inflated claims.
